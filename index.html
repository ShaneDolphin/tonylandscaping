FAQ

How often should I mow my lawn in Northern Virginia?
During spring and summer, we recommend mowing once a week, sometimes once every two weeks. Every type of grass is different, your irrigation is different, growing conditions are different, etc. In the cooler months, we stop mowing, because grass stops growing. (During this time, we recommend tree care and other hardscaping services.) We’ll help you choose the right plan for your lawn’s growth and soil type.

Can you come when I’m not home?
Absolutely. Just let us know how to access your property, and we’ll handle everything while you’re out. You’ll get a confirmation and photo log when we’re done.

Do you offer recurring services?
Yes! Most of our customers prefer monthly or seasonal service plans. They’re fully customizable, and once you set it up, we’ll take care of the rest.

What types of trees and plants do well in this region?
Northern Virginia is in USDA Zone 7—great for dogwoods, azaleas, crepe myrtles, boxwoods, and more. We’ll help you choose native and low-maintenance plants that thrive in your yard.

Do I have to be home to pay?
Nope. We offer easy, automated payments so you can focus on enjoying your space—not writing checks.
